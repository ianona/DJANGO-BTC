Is the value of Bitcoin affected by what countries accept Bitcoin as currency?

Does the legality and level of regulation of Bitcoin increase or decrease its value?

This first question aims to analyze trends within the timeline of Bitcoin as more and more countries and companies start to accept it as a form of currency. Over the course of its history, regulization and legalization has been an issue amongst different governing bodies and financial institutions. This question aims to identify any existing relationship between the weighted price of bitcoin and the occurence at which institutions accept bitcoin as a form of currency.

Data Preparation Working on the Dataset Summary Code

Finding key events

Before working on the bitcoin dataset (source: Kaggle), we must first identify key events in the history of bitcoin which pertain to its legalization or regularization across different countries or industries. To do so, we scraped an online timeline of bitcoin (https://steemit.com/cryptocurrency/@dagentlemang/bitcoin-price-and-event-timeline-points) and filtered the key events by identifying whether or not the event had any word belonging to a predetermined set of key words (words in the set included "ban","legalize","accept", among others).

Summary of data

As can be seen in the data, the legalization of bitcoin can either have a positive or negative effect in its weighted price, probably due to a number of other factors. The price of bitcoin saw a positive increase when Israel, Switzerland, and Japan announced its plans to legalize it. It also saw a decrease when Russia and India did the same thing. When China sought to ban bitcoin, it took a dip as well. However, when gaming platform Steam announced plans to accept bitcoin as payment, the weighted price saw another increase as well.

This type of data is important to understand for investors to be able to track the best time to buy and sell bitcoin with regards to the political climate. The legalization or banning of Bitcoin in developed countries could affect its price, as well as the acceptance of the currency by more and more industries. To further understand the standing of bitcoin with regards to its legalization, the researchers scraped an online website (https://www.bitcoinmarketjournal.com/bitcoin-regulation-by-country/) to identify the rate at which countries are starting to accept bitcoin.

keywords = ["ban","bans","legalizes","legalize","legal","legalized","accepts","released","introduced","launched","launch","author","authorize","shutdown"]
for i in range(len(timeline)-1,-1,-1):
    good = False
    for word in keywords:
        if word in timeline[i].get_text().lower():
            good = True

    if not good:

dates = []

keydates = []

page = requests.get("https://www.bitcoinmarketjournal.com/bitcoin-regulation-by-country/")
soup = BeautifulSoup(page.content, 'html.parser')
rows=soup.find_all("tr")
del rows[0]

countries = {}
for row in rows:
    country = row.find_all("td")[0].get_text().strip()
    legality = row.find_all("td")[1].get_text().strip()
    countries[country] = legality

legality = {}
for country in countries:
    if countries[country] not in legality:
        legality[countries[country]] = 0

    legality[countries[country]] += 1

del legality['See note']
del legality['See United States']

toFrame = {"Classifications":{},"Values":{}}
keys = tuple(legality.keys())
for i in range(0,len(list(legality.keys()))):
    toFrame["Classifications"][i] = list(legality.keys())[i]
    toFrame["Values"][i] = legality[list(legality.keys())[i]]

df = pd.DataFrame(toFrame)
print(df)
